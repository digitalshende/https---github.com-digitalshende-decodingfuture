Artificial Intelligence FAQs

Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines. It enables systems to perform tasks such as problem-solving, decision-making, and learning from experience without explicit programming.

AI is classified into three types:
1. Narrow AI (Weak AI): Designed for specific tasks (e.g., ChatGPT, Siri).
2. General AI (Strong AI): Hypothetical AI that can perform any intellectual task like a human.
3. Super AI: A theoretical concept where AI surpasses human intelligence.

1. AI: The broad field of making machines intelligent.
2. Machine Learning (ML): A subset of AI that enables machines to learn from data without being explicitly programmed.
3. Deep Learning (DL): A subset of ML that uses neural networks for complex tasks like image and speech recognition.
